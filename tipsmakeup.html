FOUNDATION

Blending techniques:

Dot and blend: Small dots of foundation to your face and blend them out using your chosen tool in circular motions.
Stippling: Use a stippling brush to gently press the foundation into your skin for a flawless finish.
Buffing: Use a buffing brush to lightly buff the foundation in circular motions to blend and remove any streaks.

Start with less and build up: Apply a thin layer of foundation and gradually add more if needed.
Blend beyond the edges: Blend your foundation down your neck and hairline to avoid a mask-like appearance.
Set your foundation: Use a setting powder to help your foundation last longer and prevent it from transferring.

Bonus tips:
Use a makeup setting spray to lock your makeup in place and prevent it from fading.
Clean your makeup brushes and sponges regularly to prevent bacteria growth.
Practice! The more you blend foundation, the better you'll become at it.

EYEBROW

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wear eyebrows:

1. Start with clean, dry brows: Make sure your eyebrows are clean and dry before you begin. You can use a spoolie brush to comb through any stray hairs and make sure they're all in the same direction.

2. Choose the right product: There are several types of eyebrow products available, including pencils, powders, and pomades. Choose the one that best suits your needs and preferences. If you have sparse brows, a brow powder or pomade might be a better choice because they're buildable and can help fill in gaps. If you have full brows, a brow pencil might be all you need to define and shape them.

3. Apply the product: Use short, light strokes to fill in your brows with the product. Start at the base of your brows and work your way outwards, following the natural shape of your brows. Be careful not to overdo it, as this can make your brows look too harsh or unnatural.

4. Blend and shape: Use a spoolie brush to blend the product into your brows and soften any harsh lines. This will also help distribute the product evenly and create a more natural-looking finish. You can also use the spoolie brush to shape your brows by combing them upwards or outwards to create a more lifted or arched appearance.

5. Set with brow gel: To keep your brows in place all day, use a clear brow gel to set them. Apply the gel to a spoolie brush and comb it through your brows, making sure all hairs are coated evenly. This will help keep your brows in place and prevent them from smudging or fading throughout the day.

6. Finish with highlighter: To add some extra dimension and definition to your brows, use a highlighter or light-colored eyeshadow on the high points of your brows (such as the brow bone and arch). This will help make your brows appear more lifted and defined, as well as create a more polished finish overall.

Here are some additional tips for specific eyeliner styles:

1. For winged eyeliner: Start by drawing a thin line along your upper lash line. Then, extend the line outwards at an angle from the outer corner of your eye. Fill in the triangle you've created with eyeliner.

For cat eye eyeliner: Similar to winged eyeliner, but the line extends further out and upwards.

EYESHADOW

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wear eyeshadow:

1. Prep your lids: Start by applying an eyeshadow primer to your lids. This will help your eyeshadow stay in place for longer and prevent creasing or smudging.

2. Choose your colors: Decide on the colors you want to use for your eyeshadow look. You can choose a neutral palette for a natural look, or go bold with bright or metallic shades.

3. Apply a base color: Use a fluffy brush to apply a light-colored eyeshadow all over your lids as a base. This will help create a smooth canvas for the other colors you'll be applying.

4. Add depth: Use a darker color to add depth and dimension to your eyes. Apply this color to the crease of your eyelid, using a windshield wiper motion with your brush. Blend well to avoid harsh lines.

5. Highlight: Use a light-colored eyeshadow to highlight the inner corners of your eyes and the brow bone. This will help brighten and open up your eyes.

6. Add definition: Use a small, dense brush to apply a darker color along the lash line, close to the roots of your lashes. This will help define and thicken the appearance of your lashes.

7. Blend everything together: Use a clean fluffy brush to blend all the colors together, making sure there are no harsh lines or patches of color. This will help create a seamless, cohesive look.

8. Finish with eyeliner and mascara: To complete the look, apply eyeliner along your upper lash line and tightline (apply eyeliner along the waterline). Finish with mascara on both upper and lower lashes.

9. Set with setting spray: To ensure your makeup lasts all day, set it with a setting spray after you're done applying everything else. This will help prevent any smudging or fading throughout the day.

BLUSHER

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wear blusher:

1. Start with clean, moisturized skin: Make sure your skin is clean and moisturized before applying blusher. This will help the blusher blend more easily and evenly.

2. Choose the right shade: Select a blusher shade that complements your skin tone. If you have fair skin, go for a light pink or peachy shade. For medium to dark skin tones, try a deeper berry or bronze hue.

3. Apply to the apples of your cheeks: Use a fluffy blush brush to apply the blusher to the apples of your cheeks, which are the roundest and most prominent parts of your cheeks when you smile.

4. Blend well: Use circular motions to blend the blusher into your skin, making sure there are no harsh lines or patches of color. You can also use a clean brush to blend out any excess product around the edges of your cheeks.

5. Build up gradually: Start with a small amount of blusher and build up gradually, adding more as needed until you achieve the desired level of color. This will help prevent overdoing it and ending up with too much blush on your cheeks.

6. Highlight: Use a highlighter or light-colored eyeshadow on the high points of your cheeks (such as the cheekbones and temples) to add extra dimension and definition to your face. This will help create a more sculpted and lifted appearance overall.

7. Finish with setting powder: To ensure your blush stays in place all day, use a setting powder to set it in place after applying it. This will help prevent any smudging or fading throughout the day.
